Yuan Tan

Fudan University, Department of Physics
China, Shanghai

I am a Ph.D. candidate in computational biophysics, supervised by Prof. Guanghong Wei. My research interest goes from computational modelling and simulation to machine learning. I have worked in the past on questions about phase separation in biological systems, pathogenic aggregation caused by desease-related mutations and functional self-assembling novel biomaterials, combining multiscale physical models (coarse-grained/all-atom) with various sampling methods (Monte Carlo/Langevin dynamics/molecular dynamics). Recently, I am using machine learning methods to predict synthesis properties of metal-organic frame materials, to investigate conformational ensembles of Intrinsically Disordered Proteins and to more efficiently remap from coarse-grained to all-atom conformations. This is my CV.
